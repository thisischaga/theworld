Green Technology to Combat Climate Change

The shift to a low-carbon economy relies heavily on technological innovation: renewables, storage, carbon capture and energy efficiency are concrete levers to reduce emissions. In 2026, several solutions demonstrate maturity and potential impact.

Large-scale renewables

Solar and wind costs continue to fall thanks to improved yields and economies of scale. Smart integration via microgrids and coupling with storage address intermittency.

Storage and grid flexibility

Advances in batteries, green hydrogen and thermal storage strengthen grid resilience. Long-duration storage and demand-side management are essential for massive renewables integration.

Carbon capture, utilization and storage

CUS technologies (direct air capture, geological sequestration and industrial reuse) are progressing. Cost reductions and supportive regulations accelerate pilot projects.

Sectoral innovation and efficiency

Energy efficiency in industry, buildings and transport reduces overall energy demand. Electrification of end uses and decarbonization of industrial processes are priorities.

In conclusion, green technologies provide powerful tools to limit climate change. Deployment requires political will, investment and cross-sector collaboration to ensure a fair and rapid transition.
